The "Black Box" of AI SEO Explained

Why traditional SEO metrics don't correlate with visibility in generative AI responses.

We recently worked with a leading SaaS productivity tool—let’s call them "Client X."

Client X was a traditional SEO powerhouse. They ranked #1 on Google for "best project management software." Their Domain Authority (DA) was 85+. Their backlink profile was pristine. Yet, when we ran their brand through the Aseon platform to test visibility in Claude and Gemini, the results were shocking.

The AI didn't recommend them. It recommended their competitor—a company with half the web traffic.

Why the Disconnect?

We opened the "Black Box" to find out. The disconnect lay in Sentiment Analysis and Contextual Nuance.

  • The SEO View: Client X had great keywords and links.
  • The LLM View: Client X had a high volume of user discussions on Reddit and technical forums complaining about a "steep learning curve."

Because LLMs prioritize the context of discussions over the volume of links, the models determined that for a generic request ("recommend a tool"), the competitor was the "safer" answer.

The Fix

Using Aseon’s Prompt Analysis, Client X didn't change their SEO strategy; they changed their documentation. They rewrote their onboarding guides to be more LLM-friendly and encouraged a campaign to generate positive, specific technical reviews.

The Result: Within 3 months, their citation frequency in "Best of" prompts increased by 240%. SEO metrics ignore sentiment; LLMs live by it.
